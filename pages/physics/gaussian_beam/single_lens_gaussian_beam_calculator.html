Single-Lens Gaussian Beam Calculator

Enter the incident beam waist position, waist radius, wavelength, and lens focal length. The app scans the lens position and calculates the new waist radius and new waist position after an ideal thin lens. Beam radius is defined as the 1/e² intensity radius.

Note: This page uses an ideal thin-lens model and an ideal TEM00 Gaussian beam. It does not include M², aberrations, aperture clipping, or refractive-index effects. If your measured beam size is an FWHM diameter or a 1/e² diameter, convert it to the 1/e² radius before entering it here.
