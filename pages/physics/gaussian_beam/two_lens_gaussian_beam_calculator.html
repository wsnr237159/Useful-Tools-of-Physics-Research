Two-Lens Gaussian Beam Calculator

Enter the incident waist, wavelength, and two focal lengths. The app scans the two lens positions and draws heatmaps for the final waist radius and final waist position after the second thin lens. Beam radius is defined as the 1/e² intensity radius.

Input Parameters

Scan Lens Positions

Used only when the lens-order constraint is enabled.

Query Lens Positions

Query Result Run calculation first, then query two lens positions.

Inverse Solver

This is a damped Newton/Levenberg nonlinear equation solver. It does not scan a grid. It solves the two equations wnew = target w and znew = target z from the initial guess, constrained to the current scan ranges.

Note: This page uses ideal thin lenses and an ideal TEM00 Gaussian beam. It does not include M², aberrations, aperture clipping, finite lens diameter, or refractive-index effects. If your measured beam size is an FWHM diameter or a 1/e² diameter, convert it to the 1/e² radius before entering it here.
